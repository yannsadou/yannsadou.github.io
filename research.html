Cryogenic instrumentation for dark matter & CMB

At Caltech, I contribute to the design and integration of cryogenic hardware for dark-matter detectors and for the BICEP Array CMB experiment. My work focuses on mechanical and thermal robustness of detector modules and their interfaces to sub-Kelvin cryogenic stages.

  • Design and sizing of Belleville washer stacks to maintain controlled preload across cooldowns.
  • Mechanical interfaces between wafers, modules, and cryostat structures.
  • Thermal pathways and minimization of parasitic heat loads in complex assemblies.
  • Integration, assembly procedures, and documentation for repeatable cryostat configuration.

Topics & methods

Sub-Kelvin cryogenics Detector packaging Thermo-mechanical modeling Precision assembly SolidWorks & FEA
Long-term goal: contribute to robust, scalable instrumentation for future flagship dark-matter and CMB missions, where mechanical details at the millimeter scale can drive mission-level performance.

Hybrid surface power systems for lunar & Martian infrastructures

My PhD at Politecnico di Milano focuses on the architecture and modeling of hybrid surface power systems for large-scale lunar and Martian infrastructures. I am interested in how microgrids, PMAD, advanced storage, and ISRU-driven power flows can be orchestrated as a coherent system.

  • Definition of reference scenarios for lunar and Martian surface infrastructures.
  • Digital twin–driven exploration of power architectures and control strategies.
  • Integration of multiple generation sources, storage technologies, and loads.
  • Resilience, redundancy, and scalability at the settlement level.

Research angles

  • How much hybridization is optimal under realistic mission and environmental constraints?
  • What are the trade-offs between redundancy, mass, and complexity?
  • How should ISRU-derived power be integrated in early vs. mature settlements?

Mission architecture, trajectories & EDL

Through ESA’s SEEDS program and related work, I have contributed to conceptual design for human Mars landers and space station elements, including trajectory and EDL trade studies.

  • High-level mission architecture for human Mars missions.
  • Exploration of inflatable EDL systems for large mass delivery to the surface.
  • Coupling of trajectory, aerodynamics, and systems constraints.

Broader interests

Across all these topics, I am especially interested in work that connects rigorous modeling, hardware realism, and mission-level thinking:

  • Digital twins as a bridge between engineering detail and mission architecture.
  • Interfaces between mechanical, thermal, electrical, and operational perspectives.
  • Design cultures that remain grounded in real manufacturing and integration constraints.
