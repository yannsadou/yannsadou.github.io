About

I am a French space systems engineer with a background spanning mechanical engineering, energy systems, and space systems design. My work lives at the interface between hardware (cryogenic structures, detector modules, power hardware) and systems-level architecture (digital twins, trajectories, and mission-level optimization).

At Caltech, I have been deeply involved in the development of cryogenic instrumentation for dark-matter searches and for the BICEP Array CMB experiment, contributing to mechanical and thermal design, integration of detector modules, Belleville washer stacks, and cryostat optimization down to sub-Kelvin temperatures.

At Politecnico di Milano, my PhD focuses on hybrid surface power systems for future lunar and Martian infrastructures, combining microgrids, PMAD, advanced energy storage, and ISRU into digital twin–driven architectures for large-scale off-world settlements.
